Everything you need to know about ASPIRE and how it works for BSCpE students.

ASPIRE (Analytics Systems for Predictive Insights and Refined Educational Outcomes) is an ML-powered platform that maps BSCpE student grades to ABET Student Outcomes, predicts future academic performance, and generates skills-based resumes.

ASPIRE primarily uses XGBoost for performance forecasting and Scikit-learn pipelines for classification and regression tasks. The backend is built on FastAPI with a React dashboard for visualization.

Yes. All student records are anonymized and stored securely. The system uses SR-CODE identifiers rather than personal identifying information, and access is role-restricted per university policy.

ABET Student Outcomes (a–l) define the competencies BSCpE graduates must demonstrate. ASPIRE automatically aligns each course's ILOs to these outcomes, tracking your proficiency level across all semesters.

Absolutely. The Resume Generator synthesizes your ABET outcome scores and strongest skill areas into a downloadable resume, highlighting competencies rather than just academic grades.
